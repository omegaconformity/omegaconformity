Technical Inspection & Methodology

Why inspection matters and how OMEGA approaches conformity assessment.

Why Inspect?

Inspection verifies that a product matches its technical design and complies with essential health and safety requirements (EHSR). It mitigates risk, prevents market recalls, and ensures consumer safety.

How We Do It

Our logic follows strict regulatory frameworks. We analyze technical files, perform visual inspections, review test reports (EMC, LVD, etc.), and verify labeling/marking requirements according to EU standards.

Documentation Quality

We focus on the Technical File—the backbone of CE Marking. We ensure Declaration of Conformity (DoC), Instruction Manuals, and Risk Assessments are structured to survive market surveillance audits.

Core EU Directives Overview

Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC)

Covers the safety requirements for machinery, interchangeable equipment, and safety components. Focuses on mechanical hazards, control systems, and risk mitigation.

Low Voltage Directive (2014/35/EU)

Applies to electrical equipment within specific voltage limits (50-1000V AC / 75-1500V DC). It ensures protection against electric shock, heat, and radiation.

EMC Directive (2014/30/EU)

Ensures that electrical and electronic equipment does not generate, or is not affected by, electromagnetic disturbance. Crucial for functional stability in modern environments.

Ecodesign & Energy Labelling

Regulations (2009/125/EC) setting mandatory requirements for the energy performance of energy-related products sold in the EU market.
