Our Testing Methodology

Why Testing Matters

Testing is not just a regulatory requirement; it is the vital foundation of product reliability.

The Core Purpose of Testing

Testing provides empirical data, confirming that a product performs its intended function without posing hazards. It serves two main functions:

  • Verification: Proof that essential safety requirements are met (e.g., that electrical insulation prevents shock).
  • Risk Mitigation: Identification of potential failure modes before the product enters the global supply chain.

How OMEGA Approaches Testing

We analyze the "Technical File" and identify the mandatory test standards (EN/ISO) applicable to the product’s scope. Our technical verification process includes:

  • Technical File Audit: Confirming all supporting test reports from accredited labs are complete and valid.
  • Gap Analysis: Identifying missing verification data required for specific directives.
  • Witness Testing Support: Providing expertise during critical test phases.

Regulatory Directives Overview

Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC)

Essential safety requirements for machinery, safety components, and related parts. Requires rigorous risk assessment and technical verification of control systems.

Low Voltage Directive (2014/35/EU)

Ensures electrical equipment within specified voltage limits provides high protection against shocks, fires, and mechanical hazards.

EMC Directive (2014/30/EU)

A mandatory requirement ensuring that electrical and electronic equipment operates as intended in its electromagnetic environment without disturbance.

Ecodesign & Energy Labelling (2009/125/EC)

Defines minimum energy efficiency and labeling requirements for energy-related products sold on the EU market.
